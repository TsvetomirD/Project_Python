Introduction

What is MCL60?

The McLaren MCL60 is a Formula One car designed and constructed by McLaren, initially under the direction of James Key, to compete in the 2023 Formula One World Championship.

Technical information

Engine Specifications

Manufacturer - Mercedes-AMG High Performance Powertrains.

Engine Name - Mercedes-AMG F1 M14 E Performance.

Type - 1.6-liter V6 turbocharged hybrid.

Hybrid System - The power unit includes a sophisticated Energy Recovery System (ERS), combining - MGU-K (Motor Generator Unit-Kinetic) - Recovers energy during braking. MGU-H (Motor Generator Unit-Heat) - Recovers energy from exhaust heat.

Fuel - The engine runs on a specific fuel blend that includes bio-components, aligning with F1's sustainability initiatives.

Battery - Stores electrical energy recovered by the MGU-K and MGU-H for additional power boost.

Front Wing & Rear Wing

Multi-element Configuration: The MCL60's front wing features multiple elements (planes) that can be adjusted to fine-tune the aerodynamic balance.

Endplates: The endplates help to control airflow around the front tires, reducing drag and optimizing the car‚Äôs aerodynamic efficiency.

Y250 Vortex: The area around 250 mm from the car's centerline (Y250) is crucial in generating vortices that help seal the floor of the car, improving overall downforce.

Main Plane and Flaps: The main plane and adjustable flaps allow for tuning of the aerodynamic load. The angle of attack can be modified based on track conditions.

Endplates: The rear wing endplates help to manage the airflow and reduce turbulence, improving the aerodynamic efficiency of the car.

DRS Mechanism: The DRS opens a flap in the rear wing, reducing drag and allowing for higher speeds on straights.

The front wing and the rear wing are constructed from lightweight yet strong materials like carbon fiber composites, ensuring durability and minimal weight.

Tires & brakes

Supplier - Pirelli - The McLaren MCL60, like all Formula 1 cars in the 2023 season, uses Pirelli tires. Pirelli provides different tire compounds for varying track conditions and strategies.

Supplier - Brembo

Brake Discs Made from carbon fiber-reinforced carbon (carbon-carbon) material, which offers excellent performance at high temperatures and is lightweight.

Brake Pads: Also made from carbon-carbon composite, designed to work in harmony with the discs.

Brake Calipers: Made from lightweight aluminum alloy, with multiple pistons to ensure even pressure distribution on the brake pads.

Brake-by-Wire System: This system electronically controls the rear brakes, allowing for more precise braking and energy recovery.

Side pod

Purpose - Aerodynamics - Manage airflow to optimize downforce and reduce drag. Cooling - House radiators and cooling systems to maintain engine and system temperatures.

Design Features - Shape and Contours - Sculpted to channel airflow efficiently.

Air Intakes - Optimized to direct cool air to radiators.

Cooling Systems - Advanced radiators and intercoolers inside.

Aerodynamic Elements - Includes vortex generators and bargeboards to enhance airflow.

Impact - Downforce and Drag - Improved cornering and straight-line performance.

Cooling Efficiency - Ensures reliable operation under race conditions.
